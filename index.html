Key Findings

  • Massachusetts hospitals face 3.12× higher odds of High-Risk vs. Western region (H=90.10, p<0.001)
  • Hip & Knee Replacement OR 2.84 vs. COPD — surgical conditions need separate HRRP pathways
  • 3 features predict 82.6% of High-Risk classifications: readmission rate, condition, geography
  • K-Means (k=4) identified Challenged Performers (20.6%) as highest-return intervention target

Analytical Methods

  • Logistic Regression: AUC 0.724, Sensitivity 70.6% — interpretable Odds Ratios for CMS policymakers
  • Decision Tree (depth 6): AUC 0.737, Sensitivity 72.1% — EHR-embeddable decision rules
  • K-Means Clustering: Silhouette 0.41 — validated manual tier structure, identified 4 performance clusters
  • Kruskal-Wallis + Mann-Whitney U: Formally confirmed geographic ERR variation is statistically decisive

05 / Publication

Peer-Reviewed Research

📄 Journal of Health Science and Medical Research (JHSMR) · Vol. 42, No. 5 · 2024 · Scopus Indexed
Echocardiography Evaluation of the Left Ventricular Mass Index (LVMI) and Geometrical Pattern in Pre-Eclampsia and Pregnancy-Induced Hypertension Patients
Thirumurugan E, Edwin Dias, Karthick R, Kalpana Devi H, Kaza Revanth Kumar, Kamaleshwari K, Karunya A, Manisha S, Madhumitha R
Objective: To evaluate the degree of hypertrophy and its geometrical patterns in hypertensive pregnant women, with or without proteinuria. Methods: Data from 88 pregnant women analyzed consecutively. Both clinical and echocardiographic data between normotensive and hypertensive women were compared. Results: Hypertensive women, with and without proteinuria, have significantly higher LV mass indexed by height than normotensive women (108.1±64.6 vs. 76.5±24.8 vs. 68.7±17.1 g/m, p=0.001). Conclusion: Women with pre-eclampsia and pregnancy-induced hypertension show significant subclinical remodelling of the left ventricle compared to normotensive pregnant women.
DOI: 10.31584/jhsmr.20241048
